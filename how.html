Arthritis is inflammation of the joints that causes pain, swelling, and stiffness. It can affect anyone but is most common in older adults, athletes, and individuals with genetic predisposition.

The two most common types are osteoarthritis (wear-and-tear) and rheumatoid arthritis (autoimmune). Left untreated, arthritis can severely restrict movement and lead to permanent joint damage.
